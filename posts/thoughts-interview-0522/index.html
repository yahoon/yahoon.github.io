The Whole Story#

I’ve attended several interviews recently, one of which sparked some deep self-reflection.

Had an interview yesterday, and the interviewer asked me:

“What have you been up to lately?”
“Organizing my past.”
“Is there really that much to organize?”
“Nearly twenty years of career accumulation—how could there not be?”

Even looking just at my last 12+ years at Red Hat, I’m still far from finishing sorting through everything I’ve gained.

For the past two decades, my career has been a non-stop relay: leaving one company one day, and starting at the next the very next morning. Especially as an operations/SRE professional, there were rarely more than three consecutive days where I wasn’t looking at something work-related.

I’ve always been someone with broad interests. I’m curious about everything and want to learn it all, but now, that has left me with a bit of “choice paralysis.” Should I stick with tech, or pivot to a brand-new track?

  If I stay in tech: It’s time to recharge—re-examine my knowledge gaps and intentionally close them.

  If I switch directions: Do I dive into content creation (blogger, vlogger, tiktoker), or explore a completely new industry? Even within content creation, there are dozens of directions to consider: Do I stick to tech content, or pivot toward something more humanistic?

There are ten thousand paths to take… but right now, it mostly feels like wandering without a clear map.

My current plan, however, is to keep sharpening my skills and continue seeking opportunities along the technical path. At the same time, I’m keeping an open mind to embrace new things and make fresh explorations.

If you find yourself in a similar phase of your career, let’s navigate this fog and grow together!
